STMD Neurons as Switched Systems with Dwell-Time Constraints

ECE 519: Modelling of Cyber-Physical Systems • Switched/Hybrid Systems • Stability via Dwell Time
2025
  • Modeled Small-Target Motion Detector (STMD) selective attention as a switching signal in a multi-agent pursuit problem, forming a continuous-time switched system with multiple equilibria.
  • Connected optic-flow saliency to a winner-take-all selector: ySTMD,1 = max(Q̇(γ)) and ySTMD,2 = arg max(Q̇(γ)), which induces switching σ(t) between target-tracking modes.
  • Reproduced the paper’s qualitative stability trend by enforcing a minimum dwell-time / ADT-style constraint to prevent rapid switching (“chattering”).
  • My contribution: replaced the paper’s nonlinear unicycle model with a CPS-friendly linear single-integrator pursuit model while preserving STMD-style switching and dwell-time regulation.
  • Simulation setup (my model): N = 5 agents, T = 30 s, Δt = 0.01 s, gain k = 0.5, and dwell-time threshold τmin = 1 s.
  • Key results: dwell-time enforcement produced smoother, more cohesive trajectories, longer mode-holding plateaus in σ(t), and a sawtooth dwell-time clock that resets at each switch—confirming correct dwell-time implementation.
Switched Systems Hybrid CPS Dwell Time / ADT STMD (Max/Argmax) Multi-Agent Stability Simulation
